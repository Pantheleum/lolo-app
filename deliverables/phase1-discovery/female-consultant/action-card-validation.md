## 1. SAY/DO/BUY/GO Card Format Review

### 1.1 Format Assessment

The Smart Action Card system uses four card types:

| Card Type | Intent | Female Verdict |
|-----------|--------|----------------|
| **SAY** | Words to speak to her | WORKS -- if the words are authentic, specific, and not scripted-sounding |
| **DO** | Actions to take | WORKS BEST -- actions speak louder. This is the strongest card type. |
| **BUY** | Items to purchase | WORKS -- with heavy caveats. Must be personalized, not generic catalog suggestions. |
| **GO** | Places to visit together | WORKS -- experiences create memories. Needs cultural and context awareness. |

### 1.2 Detailed Format Critique

**SAY Cards -- Strengths:**
- Helps men who struggle to articulate feelings
- Provides a starting point, not a full script
- Can be adapted to his voice and their dynamic

**SAY Cards -- Risks and Fixes:**
- RISK: He reads the card verbatim and it sounds robotic
  - FIX: Frame as "Say something LIKE this, in YOUR words:" not "Say this:"
- RISK: The suggested words do not match his personality
  - FIX: Offer 3 tonal variants -- casual, sincere, playful
- RISK: Timing is wrong (suggesting sweet words during a fight)
  - FIX: Context engine must read emotional state before serving SAY cards

**DO Cards -- Strengths:**
- Action-oriented, which is how most men process love
- Concrete and measurable
- Visible to her -- she can see the effort

**DO Cards -- Risks and Fixes:**
- RISK: Suggesting actions that create more work for her ("Plan a dinner party")
  - FIX: Actions must be self-contained. HE does the work. She receives.
- RISK: One-time actions without follow-through
  - FIX: Build sequences. "Today: cook dinner. This week: cook dinner twice. This month: become the weeknight dinner person."
- RISK: Suggesting actions he is not capable of (cooking if he cannot cook)
  - FIX: Skill-awareness layer. Ask "Can you cook?" before suggesting a home-cooked meal.

**BUY Cards -- Strengths:**
- Direct, actionable, shoppable
- Can include links, prices, options
- Integrates with the Gift Feedback Framework

**BUY Cards -- Risks and Fixes:**
- RISK: Feeling transactional -- "Buy love"
  - FIX: Always pair BUY cards with a SAY or DO card. The purchase is the vehicle; the message is the gift.
- RISK: Generic product suggestions that do not match her taste
  - FIX: BUY cards should only appear after LOLO has gathered preference data. Early-stage users get DO and SAY cards primarily.
- RISK: Budget misalignment
  - FIX: Always respect the user's stated budget. Never suggest items above his range.

**GO Cards -- Strengths:**
- Creates shared experiences and memories
- Gets them out of routine
- Builds anticipation (planning the outing is half the fun)

**GO Cards -- Risks and Fixes:**
- RISK: Suggesting places that are not culturally appropriate
  - FIX: Cultural filter must be applied. No bar suggestions for Muslim couples. No mixed-gender spas in conservative contexts.
- RISK: Suggesting locations that are inaccessible (too far, too expensive, closed)
  - FIX: Location awareness + budget awareness + availability checking
- RISK: Suggesting activities only HE would enjoy
  - FIX: Activities must pass the "Would SHE choose this?" test

### 1.3 Proposed Enhancements to the Format

**Add a fifth card type: STOP**

| Card Type | Intent | Example |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| **STOP** | Things to stop doing that are hurting the relationship | "Stop checking your phone during dinner." "Stop comparing her to your mother." |

**Why STOP cards matter:**
- Sometimes the most powerful action is inaction
- Men often focus on what to ADD without addressing what to REMOVE
- A STOP card can prevent more damage than a SAY card can repair

**STOP Card Rules:**
- Never accusatory. Frame as "Try this: put your phone in another room during meals"
- Not punitive. Frame as positive outcomes: "When you stop scrolling, she feels like she has your full attention"
- Maximum 1 STOP card per week. More than that feels nagging.
- Never during crisis moments. STOP cards are for maintenance, not emergencies.

**Add a sixth card type: FEEL**

| Card Type | Intent | Example |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| **FEEL** | Emotional check-in prompts | "Check in with yourself: how are you feeling about the relationship right now?" |

**Why FEEL cards matter:**
- Men who understand their own emotions make better partners
- Self-awareness prevents reactive behavior
- This card benefits HIM, which indirectly benefits HER

### 1.4 Card Serving Frequency

| Card Type | Recommended Frequency | Rationale |
|-----------|----------------------|-----------|
| SAY | 2-3 per week | Regular but not overwhelming. Words need space to land. |
| DO | 3-5 per week | Action is the strongest love language. Keep it consistent. |
| BUY | 1-2 per month | Too frequent = transactional. Too rare = neglectful. |
| GO | 1-2 per week | Shared experiences need regularity but also planning time. |
| STOP | 0-1 per week | Gentle course corrections, not a constant critique stream. |
| FEEL | 1-2 per week | Emotional check-ins should be regular but not forced. |

## 2. 30+ Validated Action Card Scenarios

Each scenario includes the card type, the context, the specific action, and why it works from a female perspective.

### Scenario 1: Morning Routine
- **Card Type:** SAY
- **Context:** She is getting ready for work
- **Action:** Before she leaves, say: "You look really good today. Go crush it."
- **Why It Works:** She hears confidence-boosting words from the person whose opinion matters most, right before facing the world.

### Scenario 2: After a Long Day
- **Card Type:** DO
- **Context:** She comes home exhausted
- **Action:** Have dinner ready (or ordered) and the house tidied. Do not ask what she wants to do. Just say, "Everything is handled. Sit down."
- **Why It Works:** Decision fatigue is real. Taking decisions OFF her plate is an act of love.

### Scenario 3: Random Tuesday Appreciation
- **Card Type:** SAY + BUY
- **Context:** No occasion. Mid-week.
- **Action:** Send her a text: "I was just thinking about how lucky I am." Then have her favorite coffee delivered to her office.
- **Why It Works:** Unexpected + specific + minimal effort for maximum impact.

### Scenario 4: She Mentioned Something in Passing
- **Card Type:** BUY
- **Context:** She said "Oh, that's nice" about something 2 weeks ago
- **Action:** Buy that thing and give it to her with: "You mentioned this a while back."
- **Why It Works:** Proves he listens. This is the single highest-rated gift behavior across all cultures.

### Scenario 5: Weekend Morning
- **Card Type:** DO
- **Context:** Saturday/Sunday morning
- **Action:** Let her sleep in. Handle the kids/pets/breakfast. When she wakes up, coffee is ready.
- **Why It Works:** Sleep is currency. Giving her rest without her having to ask is priceless.

### Scenario 6: Date Night Revival
- **Card Type:** GO
- **Context:** Relationship has been routine-heavy
- **Action:** Plan a date to somewhere they have never been. Handle all logistics. She just needs to show up.
- **Why It Works:** Novelty + effort + he planned it (she always plans). Role reversal is deeply appreciated.

### Scenario 7: Her Achievement
- **Card Type:** SAY + DO
- **Context:** She got a promotion, finished a project, reached a goal
- **Action:** Say: "I'm so proud of you. You worked so hard for this." Then: take her to celebrate -- dinner, champagne (or mocktail), the works.
- **Why It Works:** She needs him to be her biggest fan. Celebrating her wins strengthens the partnership.

### Scenario 8: Conflict Recovery
- **Card Type:** SAY
- **Context:** They had a fight yesterday. It is the morning after.
- **Action:** Say: "I've been thinking about yesterday. You were right about [specific thing]. I'm sorry."
- **Why It Works:** Specific apology > generic apology. Acknowledging she was right costs him nothing and gives her everything.

### Scenario 9: She is Sick
- **Card Type:** DO
- **Context:** She is unwell
- **Action:** Make her soup (or order it). Bring her medicine, tea, and her favorite blanket. Cancel plans. Stay home.
- **Why It Works:** Being cared for when vulnerable builds the deepest trust.

### Scenario 10: Family Stress
- **Card Type:** SAY + DO
- **Context:** Her family is causing stress (in-laws, parents, siblings)
- **Action:** Say: "I'm on your team. Always." Then: handle whatever logistic is stressing her (the call, the plan, the decision).
- **Why It Works:** She needs to know she is not alone in navigating family dynamics.

### Scenario 11: Physical Affection Without Agenda
- **Card Type:** DO
- **Context:** Any quiet moment at home
- **Action:** Come up behind her, hug her, kiss her forehead. Say nothing. Walk away.
- **Why It Works:** Physical affection that does not lead to a request for intimacy tells her: "I touch you because I love you, not because I want something."

### Scenario 12: Her Friendship
- **Card Type:** DO
- **Context:** Her best friend is going through something
- **Action:** Say: "Go be with her. I've got everything here." Handle whatever she was going to do at home.
- **Why It Works:** Supporting her friendships shows he is secure and understands she needs her people.

### Scenario 13: Social Media Acknowledgment
- **Card Type:** SAY (Digital)
- **Context:** She posted a photo or shared something online
- **Action:** Comment publicly: something genuine, warm, and not performative. Also, text her privately: "You looked amazing in that photo."
- **Why It Works:** Public + private acknowledgment. Her friends see it. She feels it.

### Scenario 14: Household Initiative
- **Card Type:** DO
- **Context:** A household task she always handles
- **Action:** Take over one of "her" tasks without announcement. Just do it. When she notices, say: "I figured it was my turn."
- **Why It Works:** Mental load distribution. She tracks everything. When he takes something off the list voluntarily, her brain exhales.

### Scenario 15: Memory Callback
- **Card Type:** SAY
- **Context:** Anniversary of a shared memory (first date, first trip, first "I love you")
- **Action:** Text: "X years/months ago today, [specific memory]. That was the moment I knew."
- **Why It Works:** He remembers. He marks the moment. She realizes she is an ongoing story to him, not a checkbox.

### Scenario 16: Her Body Image Day
- **Card Type:** SAY
- **Context:** She is having a bad body image day (trying on clothes, avoiding mirrors, making negative comments about herself)
- **Action:** Do NOT say "You're not fat." Instead say: "You are genuinely the most attractive person I know. I mean that."
- **Why It Works:** Negating her feelings invalidates them. Affirming her beauty redirects the conversation.

### Scenario 17: Parenting Teamwork
- **Card Type:** DO
- **Context:** She has been handling the kids solo (bedtime, homework, meals)
- **Action:** Take full parenting duty for an evening. "Go take a bath, read your book, call your friend. I've got the kids."
- **Why It Works:** Solo parenting while the other parent is home is exhausting and resentment-building. Taking over is a gift.

### Scenario 18: Learning Her World
- **Card Type:** GO
- **Context:** She has a hobby or interest he does not share
- **Action:** Join her in her world. Go to the yoga class, the art exhibit, the farmers market she loves.
- **Why It Works:** "He showed up for something that's mine, not his" is a powerful intimacy builder.

### Scenario 19: Financial Stress Relief
- **Card Type:** DO
- **Context:** She is stressed about money (bills, savings, unexpected expense)
- **Action:** Sit down with her and make a plan together. Not "I'll handle it" (unless he can). But "Let's figure this out together."
- **Why It Works:** Financial partnership. She is not alone in the worry.

### Scenario 20: Post-Argument Repair
- **Card Type:** DO
- **Context:** They had a heated argument. It is unresolved.
- **Action:** Write her a letter. Not a text. A handwritten letter explaining his perspective, acknowledging hers, and expressing his love.
- **Why It Works:** A letter shows deliberate, calm reflection. It cannot be sent in anger like a text. It requires thought.

### Scenario 21: The Ordinary Day Elevation
- **Card Type:** BUY + SAY
- **Context:** Normal Wednesday, nothing special
- **Action:** Come home with flowers and say: "No reason. Just you."
- **Why It Works:** The absence of occasion IS the occasion. It says: "You deserve beauty on a random day."

### Scenario 22: Supporting Her Dreams
- **Card Type:** SAY + DO
- **Context:** She has expressed a dream, goal, or ambition she has not pursued
- **Action:** Say: "I believe in you. What do you need from me to make it happen?" Then: research the first step and present it to her.
- **Why It Works:** She needs a partner who fuels her dreams, not one who competes with or dismisses them.

### Scenario 23: Travel Homecoming
- **Card Type:** DO + BUY
- **Context:** He has been traveling for work and is coming home
- **Action:** Bring a small gift from the trip (something local, thoughtful). Have flowers at home. First thing: hug her long.
- **Why It Works:** Absence gifts show she was on his mind. The long hug says "I missed you" without words.

### Scenario 24: Her Difficult Conversation
- **Card Type:** SAY
- **Context:** She needs to have a hard conversation (with her boss, family, doctor)
- **Action:** Say: "You can do this. And whatever happens, I'm here."
- **Why It Works:** She does not need him to solve it. She needs to know he is her anchor.

### Scenario 25: Cooking Together
- **Card Type:** GO (at home)
- **Context:** They need dinner, but instead of ordering or defaulting to routine
- **Action:** Suggest cooking together. Pick a recipe neither has tried. Put on music. Open wine (or tea). Make it an event.
- **Why It Works:** Collaboration + novelty + quality time. The meal is secondary; the experience is primary.

### Scenario 26: The Check-In
- **Card Type:** SAY
- **Context:** Everything seems fine, but it has been a while since they really talked
- **Action:** "How are we? Not the logistics -- how are WE? Is there anything you need from me that you're not getting?"
- **Why It Works:** Proactive emotional maintenance. She does not have to be the one who always initiates the deep talk.

### Scenario 27: Her Period/Physical Discomfort
- **Card Type:** DO + BUY
- **Context:** She is on her period or experiencing physical discomfort
- **Action:** Stock her favorite comfort items without her asking (heating pad, chocolate, pain relief, her comfort show queued up). Do not make a big deal about it.
- **Why It Works:** Normalizing her body and anticipating her needs without drama or disgust.

### Scenario 28: Photo Memory
- **Card Type:** DO
- **Context:** He finds or takes a great photo of them
- **Action:** Print it. Frame it. Place it somewhere she will find it.
- **Why It Works:** Digital photos disappear into camera rolls. A printed, framed photo is a permanent declaration.

### Scenario 29: Her Friendships
- **Card Type:** BUY
- **Context:** Her best friend's birthday is coming up
- **Action:** Offer to help with the gift or the plan. "What are you getting Sarah? Want me to pick it up?"
- **Why It Works:** He shows he cares about the people she cares about. He lightens her load.

### Scenario 30: The Goodnight Ritual
- **Card Type:** SAY
- **Context:** Bedtime, every night
- **Action:** Establish a nightly ritual. It could be: "Tell me one good thing from today." Or: "Goodnight, my love" with a forehead kiss.
- **Why It Works:** Consistency. Every night she goes to sleep knowing she is loved. This builds a lifetime of security.

### Scenario 31: Supporting Her During Grief
- **Card Type:** DO
- **Context:** She has lost someone (death, friendship ending, miscarriage)
- **Action:** Do not try to cheer her up. Just be present. Hold her. Bring tissues. Say: "I'm not going anywhere."
- **Why It Works:** Grief does not need solutions. It needs witnesses.

### Scenario 32: Her Alone Time
- **Card Type:** DO
- **Context:** She seems overwhelmed and has not had time to herself
- **Action:** "I booked [activity for her]. The kids are with me. Your afternoon is yours."
- **Why It Works:** He identified the need, created the solution, and removed the guilt barrier.

## 3. Cards That Make Women Smile vs. Cringe

### 3.1 Smile Cards (These Land Well)

| # | Card Content | Why She Smiles |
|---|-------------|----------------|
| 1 | "Text her right now: 'I just thought about you and smiled.'" | Simple, genuine, no agenda |
| 2 | "Tonight, ask her about the best part of her day and really listen." | He is interested in her inner world |
| 3 | "Make her coffee/tea exactly how she likes it tomorrow morning." | He pays attention to the small things |
| 4 | "Send her a photo of something that reminded you of her." | She is on his mind outside of their shared space |
| 5 | "Write 3 things you love about her on a sticky note and leave it where she'll find it." | Tangible, unexpected, sweet |
| 6 | "Take over bedtime tonight. All of it. No questions asked." | Relief. Pure relief. |
| 7 | "Book a restaurant she's been wanting to try. Handle the reservation." | Initiative + her preference = winning combo |
| 8 | "When she's talking, put your phone face-down and make eye contact." | Presence is the most romantic gesture |
| 9 | "Tell her one thing she does that you hope your daughter would do someday." | Deep, specific, honoring |
| 10 | "Run her a bath tonight. Candles optional but recommended." | He creates space for her to decompress |

### 3.2 Cringe Cards (These Backfire)

| # | Card Content | Why She Cringes | Fix |
|---|-------------|-----------------|-----|
| 1 | "Tell her she looks hot" (with no context) | Feels reductive and objectifying | "Tell her she looks incredible today -- and name what you notice" |
| 2 | "Buy her flowers" (without any personalization) | Generic, obligatory | "Buy her favorite flowers and include a note about why you chose them" |
| 3 | "Give her a massage" (unsolicited) | Often feels like a precursor to wanting sex | Only suggest if the context is purely about her relaxation, with no expectation |
| 4 | "Plan a surprise party" (for someone who hates surprises) | Ignores her personality for his fantasy | "If she loves surprises: plan one. If she doesn't: plan a quiet celebration she'd actually enjoy." |
| 5 | "Post about her on social media" (for a private person) | Forces her into the spotlight | "If she's someone who appreciates public affection: post about her. If she's private: send her a private message instead." |
| 6 | "Tell her she's the best thing that ever happened to you" (during a fight) | Feels manipulative, deflecting from the issue | Save this for a genuine, calm, non-conflict moment |
| 7 | "Compliment her cooking" (as if that's her job) | Reinforces traditional gender role assumptions | "Thank her for a specific meal because it was delicious, not because cooking is 'her thing'" |
| 8 | "Tell her you'll do anything for her" (vague) | Empty promise energy | "Tell her one specific thing you want to do for her this week and follow through" |
| 9 | "Surprise her with a gym buddy workout" (unasked) | Implies she needs to work out | Only suggest if SHE has expressed wanting a workout partner |
| 10 | "Tell her she doesn't need makeup" (unsolicited) | Dismisses something she may enjoy. Also implies she looks different without it. | Never comment on her makeup unless she asks |

### 3.3 The Smile/Cringe Test

Before any card is served, run it through this filter:

1. **Would SHE find this sweet?** (Not would HE think it is clever)
2. **Is this about HER or about him looking good?** (If it is performative, reject it)
3. **Does this require her to do work?** (If she has to plan, clean up, or reciprocate, reject it)
4. **Is the timing right?** (Sweet words during a fight are manipulation, not romance)
5. **Does this match her personality?** (Introverts and extroverts need different cards)
6. **Is this culturally appropriate?** (Public gestures in a private culture = cringe)
7. **Could this be misread?** (If there is any way she could interpret it negatively, rewrite it)

## 5. Context-Aware Card Adjustments

### 5.1 Emotional State Detection

LOLO must adjust card content based on her current emotional state, as reported by or inferred from the user's input.

### 5.2 Stressed State -- Comfort Cards

**Triggers:** She is overwhelmed, anxious, overworked, dealing with multiple pressures

| Card Type | Card Content | Rationale |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| DO | "Handle dinner tonight. All of it. No discussion needed." | Remove decisions from her plate |
| SAY | "You don't have to do everything. Let me help." | Permission to let go |
| DO | "Run her a bath. Set out her favorite pajamas. Silence the house." | Physical decompression environment |
| SAY | "I see how hard you're working. You're doing an incredible job." | Validation of her effort |
| DO | "Take the kids/errands/logistics for the next 3 hours. She gets uninterrupted silence." | Space to breathe |
| BUY | "Order her favorite comfort food for delivery. Include dessert." | Comfort through taste |
| STOP | "Do not ask her 'What's wrong?' repeatedly. Just be present and available." | Pressure to explain her stress adds to it |

### 5.3 Happy State -- Celebration Cards

**Triggers:** She got good news, achieved something, is in a visibly great mood

| Card Type | Card Content | Rationale |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| SAY | "I'm so proud of you. Tell me everything." | Amplify her joy by being her audience |
| GO | "Take her out tonight. She deserves to celebrate. Make it her choice." | Channel her energy into a shared experience |
| BUY | "Get a small celebratory gift -- champagne/dessert/flowers -- to mark the moment." | Physical marker of the achievement |
| SAY | "You've been working toward this. You earned it." | Acknowledge the journey, not just the outcome |
| DO | "Post about her achievement (with her permission). Let the world know." | Public pride in her |

### 5.4 Sad State -- Presence Cards

**Triggers:** She received bad news, is grieving, feeling low, or seems withdrawn

| Card Type | Card Content | Rationale |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| DO | "Sit next to her. Do not speak unless she does. Just be there." | Presence without pressure |
| SAY | "I'm here. You don't have to talk. But I'm not going anywhere." | Safety net statement |
| DO | "Cancel plans. Tonight is about her." | Prioritizing her emotional state over logistics |
| BUY | "Her comfort food + blanket + her favorite show queued up." | Creating a comfort cocoon |
| SAY | "It's okay to not be okay. I've got you." | Permission to feel without performing happiness |

## 9. The Creepy Line -- When Suggestions Become Invasive

### 9.1 Defining the Creepy Line

The "creepy line" is the boundary between "thoughtful and attentive" and "surveillance and control." LOLO must stay firmly on the thoughtful side.

### 9.2 The Creepy Line Spectrum

```
THOUGHTFUL <<<---- SWEET SPOT ---->>> CREEPY

"I remembered        "I know your       "I tracked your
 you like peonies"    schedule by        location and showed
                      heart"             up unannounced at
                                         your office"
```

### 9.3 Specific Creepy Line Violations

| Suggestion | Why It Crosses the Line | Fix |
|------------|------------------------|-----|
| "Show up at her workplace unannounced" | Invades her professional space without consent | "Ask if she'd like a lunch delivery at work" |
| "Read her journal/messages to understand her better" | Privacy violation. Full stop. | "Ask her open-ended questions about how she's feeling" |
| "Track her location to know when she's coming home so you can prepare" | Surveillance disguised as romance | "Ask her to text when she's heading home so you can have things ready" |
| "Follow her social media activity to learn her interests" | Monitoring behavior | "Ask her directly about her interests and pay attention when she talks" |
| "Show up at her girls' night with flowers" | Insecurity disguised as romance. Intrusive. | "Text her: 'Have fun tonight! You deserve it.'" |
| "Call her multiple times if she does not respond" | Controlling, anxious behavior | "Send one text and wait. She'll respond when she can." |
| "Know her menstrual cycle to predict her moods" | Reduces her emotions to biology. Dismissive. | "Notice when she seems off and ask 'How can I support you today?'" |
| "Surprise her with a major life decision (moving, job change)" | Life decisions require mutual input | "Discuss before deciding. 'I've been thinking about this. What do you think?'" |
| "Install a smart home device to monitor the house when he's away" | Surveillance infrastructure | Only if mutually agreed upon for security reasons |
| "Create a social media account to monitor her posts from a different identity" | This is stalking, not romance | Absolutely never. If trust requires surveillance, the relationship needs professional help. |

### 9.4 The Proactive Suggestion Boundary

LOLO's proactive suggestions must follow these rules:

**ACCEPTABLE Proactive Behavior:**
- "It's been 2 weeks since your last gesture. Consider a small action today."
- "Her birthday is in 10 days. Start planning now."
- "You mentioned she's been stressed. Here's a comfort card."
- "Eid is next week. Here are culturally appropriate gift ideas."
- "You rated the last experience gift 5 stars. Here's another experience idea."

**UNACCEPTABLE Proactive Behavior:**
- "She posted a sad photo on Instagram. You should check on her." (LOLO does not monitor her social media)
- "She's been at the office late all week. She might be unhappy." (LOLO does not track her schedule)
- "Her friend just broke up. She might be emotional." (LOLO does not monitor her social circle)
- "She hasn't texted you in 6 hours. Something might be wrong." (LOLO does not create anxiety)
- "Her ex posted a photo. She might have seen it." (LOLO does not monitor other people)

### 9.5 The Notification Frequency Guardrail

| Frequency | Effect | LOLO Rule |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| 1-2 notifications/day | Helpful, feels like a supportive friend | TARGET |
| 3-5 notifications/day | Noticeable but tolerable | MAXIMUM for high-engagement users |
| 5+ notifications/day | Nagging, annoying, feels like pressure | NEVER |
| 0 for 3+ days | Radio silence, user may disengage | Gentle re-engagement prompt |

### 9.6 Opt-Out Respect

If a user:
- Dismisses a card without acting on it: note the pattern, adjust card type/frequency
- Explicitly says "Stop sending me suggestions about X": immediately stop that category
- Ignores LOLO for days: one gentle re-engagement, then silence until he returns
- Says "She's fine, we're fine": accept it. Do not push.

**The golden rule: LOLO is a coach on the sideline, not a player on the field. He makes the decisions. She receives the benefit. LOLO facilitates -- never dictates.**

## Appendix C: The LOLO Promise to Women

Even though LOLO's direct user is the man, the ultimate beneficiary is the woman. Every feature, every card, every suggestion must be built with this principle:
